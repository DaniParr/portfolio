Actin Strand Detection Software AI

As a means of detecting the actin structure in cancer cells, we decided to use FilamentSensor Java Software by Benjamin Eltzner found in their research paper The Filament Sensor for Near Real-Time Detection of Cytoskeletal Fiber Structures to detect the actin structures in our cells. The software made use of numerous parameters and our images were vastly different in brightness and noise that one set of parameters could not fit all. By incorporating a functional AI model to make predictions on the value of the parameters we were able to dynamically produce parameters to fit diverse images.

https://github.com/DaniParr/MBio-Functional-CNN

Features

Constrained Sample Size

Having limited access to cell images, I was only able to compile a sample size of 100 images. This encouraged me to find ways to manipulate data by resizing, normalizing, and testing different weights to maximize accuracy and minimize loss.

70% Accuracy

After trial and error, I was able to ensure 70% accuracy with less than 1% loss on the training samples..
