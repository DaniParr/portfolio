Machine intelligence Lab

The Machine Intelligence Laboratory (MIL) provides a synergistic environment dedicated to the study and development of intelligent, autonomous robots. The faculty and students associated with the laboratory conduct research in the theory and realization of machine intelligence covering topics such as machine learning, real-time computer vision, statistical modeling, robot kinematics, autonomous vehicles, teleoperation and human interfaces, robot and nonlinear control, computational intelligence, neural networks, and general robotics. Applications of MIL research include autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs), autonomous water surface vehicles (ASVs), autonomous land vehicles, autonomous air vehicles (AAVs including quadcopters and micro air vehicles, MAVs) , swarm robots, humanoid robots, and autonomous household robots.

My Involvements

Under Water Image Enhancement AI

In order to improve the effectiveness of the Machine Intelligence Lab's autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV), Subjugator, it needs to be able to make accurate distinctions in its environment. With water refracting light, it can be difficult to use the raw image from the camera to distinct the types of objects in view for the UAV. Utilizing the machine learning model provided by Xuelei Chen, Pin Zhang, Lingwei Quan, Chao Yi, Cunyue Lu from their paper Underwater "Image Enhancement based on Deep Learning and Image Formation Model," we were able to successfully drive data from one of the camera topics to be enhanced and be constantly output for clearer imaging.

GMan or Bootlegger Object Detection

In Robosub 2022 and 2023, one of the tasks is to distinguish between two images. One of a Bootlegger and the other of a GMan. In order to successfully accomplish this task, the autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV) must use computer vision to distinguish between the two. Training the YOLOv5 object detection model by Ultralytics, I was able to achieve detections upwards of 90% confidence on live feeds.
